My Professional Story

My journey into engineering began not in a classroom, but through hands-on experiences that revealed how technology could solve real human problems. From family solar installations to teaching robotics, each chapter has taught me that great engineering combines technical skill with understanding people's needs.

Work Experience

January 2026 – Present
Robotics & Programming Trainer
Syrian Computer Society | Jableh, Syria

Every week, I step into a classroom filled with curious minds eager to understand how robots think. My role is more than teaching syntax - it's about showing students how to give machines purpose. I watch as their eyes light up when their first Python script works, or when a robot they programmed completes a task for the first time.

What I love most is adapting complex concepts into stories and challenges that make sense to beginners. We don't just learn programming; we solve puzzles, build small worlds, and create tools that could one day help our communities.

August 2025 – January 2026
Systems Technician
Al-Fajre Computer Center | Jableh, Syria

This role taught me that technology is deeply human. Every computer that wouldn't start, every printer that refused to print, came with a person who needed their problem solved. I learned to listen first - to understand not just what was broken, but how it affected their work or their day.

There's a particular satisfaction in teaching someone how to maintain their own system, watching them gain confidence with each lesson. The repair work taught me patience; the teaching work taught me how to break down complex ideas into simple steps anyone could follow.

February 2023 – August 2023
Robotics Project Coach
Sanad Youth Development Foundation | Lattakia, Syria

Mentoring three talented students for a national competition was like conducting a small orchestra. Each had different strengths - one excelled at mechanical design, another at programming logic, the third at creative problem-solving. My job wasn't to direct them, but to help them harmonize their skills.

We chose to build a robot that could sort recyclable materials - a project that taught them engineering while connecting to bigger questions about sustainability. The real victory wasn't just in the competition results, but in watching them learn to think like engineers who consider both technical and social impacts.
